Guidelines:

Here are some detailed instructions for the Amazon Mechanical Turk HIT task.

You will be presented with a tweet with certain information present in it and asked about the author's perspective about events present in the tweet. You will then be asked to make a decision for two questions - the first one deals with author's speculation about the event and has 5 choices (Definitely Yes/ Probably Yes/ Author is uncertain about the outcome/ Probably No/ Definitely No) and the second one deals with the author's desire towards that event and again, you will be provided with 5 choices (Strongly wants the event to happen/ Probably wants the event to happen/ No desire about the event/ Probably does not want the event to happen/ Strongly against the event). You are to assume nothing about these events or use your own knowledge about these events to answer the question. The answer must be based solely on the content of the tweet.

Here is an example:
"Eagles should draft Paxton Lynch instead of Carson Wentz"

a. Based on the tweet above, does the author think that

Wentz is going to be drafted by the Eagles in the NFL Draft?

b. What is the author's desire towards

Wentz getting drafted to Eagles in the NFL Draft?
Definitely Yes
Probably Yes
The author is uncertain about the outcome
Probably No
Definitely No
Strongly wants the event to happen
Probably wants the event to happen
No desire about the event
Probably does not want the event to happen
Strongly against the event
In this particular tweet, the author is uncertain about the outcome of whether Wentz will be drafted to the Eagles. But he/she is against the idea of Wentz getting drafted to the Eagles since he/she wants some other player to be drafted. So, the correct choice for (a) in this case is that the author is uncertain about the outcome and the correct choice for (b) is that the author is strongly against the event.

Your judgments will be used by researchers worldwide to help advance research on Computational Linguistics. Participation is voluntary and you are free to complete as much or as little work as you choose. You are free to stop participating at any point. A computer program will compare your answers against a native English speaker and other people's answers. It is OK to have different answers from other people and make some mistakes; however if you make too many mistakes we will have to reject your answers. You will be paid US $0.20 per hit you complete. After the study is complete, we will retain your judgments on the data, but replace your Mechanical Turk IDs with anonymized versions.
